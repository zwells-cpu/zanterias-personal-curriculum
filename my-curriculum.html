Becoming a Better Communicator

4 weeks · YouTube + note-taking + real-world practice

W1
The Foundation — How Communication Works
Body language, tone, and presence
▼
📺
YouTube Watch
Celeste Headlee – "10 Ways to Have a Better Conversation" (TED)
One of the most impactful 11 minutes on listening and conversation you'll find. Take notes on what surprises you.
📺
YouTube Watch
Julian Treasure – "How to Speak So People Want to Listen" (TED)
Focus on his HAIL framework — Honesty, Authenticity, Integrity, Love.
📓
Note-Taking Challenge
Create a "Communication Snapshot" page in your journal
Write: What are your current communication strengths? What do you want to change? Be honest.
🎯
Practice
Have one "intentional conversation" this week
No phone. Full eye contact. Ask one meaningful follow-up question. Note how it felt afterward.
🏆
Week 1 Challenge
Record yourself speaking for 2 minutes on any topic. Watch it back. Write 3 observations.
📝 My notes for this week
W2
Assertive, Not Aggressive
Speaking with confidence and clarity
▼
📺
YouTube Watch
Amy Cuddy – "Your Body Language May Shape Who You Are" (TED)
Pay attention to what she says about presence vs. performance.
📺
YouTube Channel
Vinh Giang – Communication & Voice Training videos
Binge 2–3 of his short videos on vocal power and how to speak so people lean in.
📓
Note-Taking Challenge
Write your "I need / I feel / I want" statements
Practice assertive language. Write 5 scenarios where you'd normally stay quiet — then write what you'd confidently say instead.
🎯
Practice
Speak up in one situation you'd normally let slide
A restaurant order, a meeting, a text response — wherever feels true to where you are right now.
🏆
Week 2 Challenge
Leave a voice memo or voice note to a friend saying something you mean. No text crutch.
📝 My notes for this week
W3
Listening as a Superpower
Active listening, empathy, and reading the room
▼
📺
YouTube Watch
Search: "Active Listening Skills – MedCircle" or "The Power of Listening – William Ury TED"
Both are excellent. Pick one or watch both — they're under 15 min each.
🎧
Podcast Episode
Dare to Lead Podcast – Brené Brown
Find any episode on empathy or difficult conversations. Listen on a walk or drive.
📓
Note-Taking Challenge
Listening audit: What am I actually doing when others talk?
Be honest. Are you planning your response? Distracted? Write a before/after comparison by week's end.
🏆
Week 3 Challenge
Have a 10-minute conversation where your only job is to listen, reflect back, and ask one open question. No advice giving.
📝 My notes for this week
W4
Put It All Together
Written communication + your communication style
▼
📺
YouTube Watch
"How to Write More Clearly" — Thomas Frank or Ali Abdaal
Communication isn't just verbal — great writers are great thinkers.
📓
Capstone Note
Write your personal "Communication Code"
1 page. What kind of communicator are you now? What are your 3 core commitments going forward?
🎯
Final Practice
Re-record yourself speaking for 2 minutes (same topic as Week 1)
Compare the two videos. Notice what has changed — even subtly.
🏆
Track 1 Capstone
Lead or facilitate one real conversation — a meeting, a family discussion, a friend group decision — using everything you've learned.
📝 My notes for this week

The Book — Self-Help by a Black Woman

4 weeks · Read + reflect + apply

📗
✦ Recommended Pick
Set Boundaries, Find Peace
by Nedra Glover Tawwab — licensed therapist & NYT bestselling author
A warm but clear-eyed guide to understanding why we struggle with boundaries and how to set them without guilt. Rooted in CBT, deeply practical, and incredibly relevant to someone navigating transitions and relationships. Alternatively: Year of Yes by Shonda Rhimes — a page-turner about saying yes to your fullest self.
W5
Part 1 — What Are Boundaries, Really?
Chapters 1–4 · Understanding the "why"
▼
📚
Reading Goal
Read Chapters 1–4 (approx. 30–45 min total, spread across the week)
Read with a highlighter or sticky notes. Mark anything that hits close to home.
📺
YouTube Supplement
Search: "Nedra Glover Tawwab interview" on YouTube
Hearing the author speak deepens the reading. Several great podcast-style interviews are available.
📓
Reflection Prompt
Where in your life do you consistently over-give or feel resentful afterward?
Write without judgment. This is data, not shame.
📝 My reading notes
W6
Part 2 — Types of Boundaries
Chapters 5–8 · Getting specific
▼
📚
Reading Goal
Chapters 5–8 — emotional, physical, time, and digital boundaries
Note which category feels most out of balance in your life right now.
📓
Reflection Prompt
Map your boundary landscape
Draw a simple diagram or list: 3 relationships or situations where boundaries feel clear, and 3 where they feel foggy or missing.
🎯
Apply It
Set one micro-boundary this week
Say no to something small. Or say yes to protecting your time. Notice how it feels before and after.
📝 My reading notes
W7
Part 3 — The Hard Ones
Chapters 9–12 · Family, work, and guilt
▼
📚
Reading Goal
Chapters 9–12 — family systems, workplace dynamics, and guilt as a tool
This section tends to hit the hardest. Read slowly.
📓
Reflection Prompt
Where does your guilt come from, and whose voice does it sound like?
Write freely. No editing. Just let it come out.
📝 My reading notes
W8
Finish + Integrate
Final chapters · Your personal takeaway
▼
📚
Reading Goal
Finish the book
Read the final chapters and sit with the ending before moving on.
📓
Capstone Note
Write a 1-page "Letter to Yourself" based on this book
What did you learn? What are you willing to change? What permission did you finally give yourself?
🏆
Track 2 Capstone
Share one thing from this book with someone in your life. A text, a conversation, a recommendation. Pass the wisdom forward.
📝 My reading notes

Project Management Foundations

4 weeks · YouTube + free tools + a real project

W9
PM 101 — How Projects Actually Work
Scope, timeline, stakeholders, and basics
▼
📺
YouTube Watch
Search: "Project Management for Beginners – Google Project Management Certificate" (Coursera YouTube clips)
Even 1–2 clips give you a solid foundation. Google's PM Certificate is free to audit on Coursera as well.
📺
YouTube Watch
"What is a Project Charter?" — any clear explainer video (under 10 min)
A project charter is the most underrated tool. Understanding it unlocks everything.
📓
Note-Taking Challenge
Define your "Personal Project" for this track
Choose something real: planning a trip, launching your notary business, preparing for fall semester. This becomes your live case study.
📝 My notes for this week
W10
Planning — Timelines, Tasks & Tools
Gantt charts, kanban, and free PM tools
▼
📺
YouTube Watch
"How to Use Notion for Project Management" or "Trello Tutorial for Beginners"
Pick one tool (Notion, Trello, or Asana — all free) and set it up for your personal project.
💻
Hands-On
Build your project board
Create a kanban or task list for your personal project. Add deadlines, milestones, and categories.
📓
Reflection
What does a "realistic" timeline look like for your project?
Map it out. Build in buffer time. Practice scope awareness — not everything needs to happen at once.
📝 My notes for this week
W11
Execution & Communication in Projects
Stakeholders, updates, and handling roadblocks
▼
📺
YouTube Watch
"Managing Stakeholders in Project Management" — any solid explainer
Think about how your communication skills (Track 1) connect directly here.
🎯
Apply It
Write a project status update — even if it's just for yourself
"Here's where I am. Here's what's on track. Here's what I need to solve." Practice the format.
📓
Reflection
Where has your personal project hit a roadblock this week?
Write the problem clearly, then brainstorm 3 possible paths forward. This is real PM work.
📝 My notes for this week
W12
Close Out + Next Steps
Lessons learned, retrospective, and what's next
▼
📺
YouTube Watch
"Project Retrospective — How to Run One" or "Lessons Learned in PM"
A retrospective is one of the most valuable project habits you can build.
📓
Capstone Note
Run a 3-question retrospective on your personal project
1) What went well? 2) What didn't? 3) What would I do differently? Be generous and honest with yourself.
🎓
Bonus Resource
Consider: Google Project Management Certificate (Coursera, free to audit)
If you loved this, the full certificate is a credentialed next step — relevant for healthcare admin, operations, and beyond.
🏆
Final Capstone
Present your personal project to one person — a friend, partner, anyone. Walk them through what you built, what you learned, what's next. That's a PM close-out.
📝 My notes for this week

Travel as Curriculum

Non-conventional learning — woven throughout the 12 weeks

These aren't vacations — they're field trips for your mind. Each connects to your three learning tracks.

🏛️

Communication: Attend a Live Talk

Find a TEDx event, local speaker series, or Toastmasters guest night in your area. Watch skilled communicators in person — body language, pacing, and energy are things YouTube can't fully teach.

🔗 Search: TEDx [your city] · eventbrite.com
☕

Book Club Field Trip

Visit an independent Black-owned bookstore if one is near you. Browse their staff picks. Buy your book there. There's something about the act of physically choosing a book that sets the tone for reading it intentionally.

🔗 Search: Black-owned bookstores near me
🌿

Nature + Note-Taking Retreat

Take yourself on a solo half-day "learning retreat." A park, a botanical garden, a waterfront. Bring your journal. No music, no podcast — just you and whatever surfaces. Some of the best reflection happens in motion.

🗓 Plan for: end of Week 4 or Week 8
🏙️

PM Field Study: Study a Space

Visit a well-run business, museum, or organized event near you. Observe how it's structured — the flow, the signage, the staff coordination. Write down what you notice as if you're the PM who designed it.

🗓 Plan for: Week 10 or 11
🎤

Community Event — Listen + Connect

Attend a panel, a workshop, or a community meeting on a topic that interests you. The goal: practice your listening and conversation skills with real strangers in a low-stakes setting.

🔗 Search: eventbrite.com · meetup.com
🎓

Campus Visit — Radiology School

If you haven't already, visit the campus or attend an open house for your radiology program this fall. Connect your learning now with your learning later. This 12-week curriculum is a bridge — make it feel real.

🗓 Plan for: sometime in the 12 weeks
